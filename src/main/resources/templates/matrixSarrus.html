Calculating the Determinant using the Sarrus Method

When you click "Generate Sarrus 5 × 3 Matrix", your initial \(3 \times 3\) matrix is extended by repeating the first two columns on the right-hand side.

Given:\[A=\begin{bmatrix}a & b & c \\d & e & f \\g & h & i\end{bmatrix}\] the Sarrus method calculates:\[\det(A)=(aei+bfg+cdh) - (ceg+bdi+afh) \] The downward diagonals form the positive total, and the upward diagonals form the negative total.

Finally, the determinant is calculated by subtracting the negative total from the positive total.
