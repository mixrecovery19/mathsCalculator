2.Calculate The Determinant of Matrix A

Why do we calculate the determinant of Matrix A?

Before we can solve a linear system using the inverse matrix method, we must first calculate the determinant of the coefficient matrix, \(A\). The determinant tells us whether the matrix has an inverse. If the determinant is zero, the inverse does not exist and this method cannot be used.

For the coefficient matrix \[A=\begin{bmatrix}a & b\\c & d\end{bmatrix}\] the determinant is calculated using \[\det(A)=ad-bc\] In matrices, we always identify elements by their row first and then column position. \[a=A_{11},\quad b=A_{12},\quad c=A_{21},\quad d=A_{22} \] The determinant is calculated in three stages: \[\text{Step 1: } a \times d\] \[\text{Step 2: } b \times c\] \[\text{Step 3: } (a \times d) - (b \times c)\] Use the walkthrough below to calculate the determinant of your coefficient matrix before continuing to calculate its inverse.
