3. Calculate the Inverse of Matrix A

Why do we calculate the inverse of Matrix A?

Now that you have calculated the determinant of the coefficient matrix, the next step is to calculate its inverse. The inverse of Matrix \(A\) allows us to solve the linear system by transforming the matrix equation \[AX=B\] into \[X=A^{-1}B\] where: \[A=\begin{bmatrix}a & b\\c & d\end{bmatrix} \quad X=\begin{bmatrix}x\\y\end{bmatrix}\qquad B=\begin{bmatrix}k_1\\k_2\end{bmatrix}\] Before we can multiply by the constants vector, we must first calculate the inverse of Matrix \(A\). For a \(2\times2\) matrix, the inverse is \[A^{-1} = \frac{1}{\det(A)}\begin{bmatrix}d & -b\\-c & a\end{bmatrix}\] This walkthrough demonstrates the process step by step: \[\text{Step 1: Swap the main diagonal values}\] \[\text{Step 2: Negate the off-diagonal values}\] \[\text{Step 3: Apply the determinant}\] Once the inverse has been calculated, you will be ready to multiply it by the constants vector to obtain the values of \(x\) and \(y\).
