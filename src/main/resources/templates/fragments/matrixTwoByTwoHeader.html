Calculating the Determinant of a 2 x 2 Matrix

The determinant helps determine whether a matrix can be inverted.
The determinant is also an important part of calculating the inverse of a matrix: \[A^{-1}=\frac{1}{ad-bc}\begin{bmatrix}d & -b \\-c & a\end{bmatrix}\] The first step in calculating the inverse of any matrix is to calculate the determinant.
Unlike a 3 × 3 matrix determinant, a 2 × 2 determinant can be calculated using a simple formula: \[ad - bc\] If the determinant is zero, the matrix is singular and does not have an inverse.
If the determinant is non-zero, you can proceed to calculate the inverse matrix.
