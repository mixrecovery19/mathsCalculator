Euclidean Algorithm Walkthrough

The Euclidean Algorithm is an efficient method for finding the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) of two positive integers.

What is the Euclidean Algorithm?

The Euclidean Algorithm repeatedly divides one number by another and replaces the pair of numbers with the divisor and the remainder.

When the remainder becomes zero, the last non-zero remainder is the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD).

This algorithm is fundamental to number theory and is widely used in cryptography, including RSA encryption.
