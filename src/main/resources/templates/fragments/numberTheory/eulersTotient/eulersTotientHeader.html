Euler's Totient Function φ(n)

Euler's Totient Function, written as \( \phi(n) \), counts how many positive integers less than or equal to n are relatively prime (coprime) to n.

What is Euler's Totient Function?

Two numbers are coprime if their greatest common divisor (GCD) is 1.

For large values of n, Euler's Totient can be calculated efficiently using the distinct prime factors of the number.

The formula is:

\[ \phi(n)= n \left(1-\frac{1}{p_1}\right) \left(1-\frac{1}{p_2}\right) \cdots \left(1-\frac{1}{p_k}\right) \]

where \(p_1,p_2,\ldots,p_k\) are the distinct prime factors of \(n\).
