Step 1 – Identify the Distinct Prime Factors

Euler's Totient Formula only requires the distinct prime factors of the number. Although a prime factor may appear multiple times in the prime factorisation, it is only used once in the formula.
Why only the distinct prime factors?

Suppose the prime factorisation of 315 is:

\(315 = 3^2 \times 5 \times 7\)

Even though 3 appears twice, Euler's Totient Formula only uses each unique prime once:

\(3,\;5,\;7\)
