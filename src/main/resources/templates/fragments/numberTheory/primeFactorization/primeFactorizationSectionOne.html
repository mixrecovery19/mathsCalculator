How We Begin

We begin by testing the smallest prime number: \[2\] If the number divides evenly by \(2\), then \(2\) is one of its prime factors.
In general, we are looking for prime numbers that divide the current value with no remainder: \[\text{Current Number} \div \text{Prime Factor} = \text{Whole Number}\] If there is a remainder, we move to the next prime number: \[2,\ 3,\ 5,\ 7,\ 11,\ 13,\ 17,\ldots \]
