Finding Prime Factors Using Repeated Division

Prime factorization expresses a positive integer as a product of prime numbers.
Rather than guessing the factors, we repeatedly divide by the smallest prime number that divides evenly into the current value.
For example, \[84 = 2 \times 2 \times 3 \times 7\] or \[84 = 2^{2}\times3\times7\] Throughout this walkthrough you will:
• Enter any positive integer between 2 and 10,000.
• Test successive prime numbers for divisibility.
• Divide whenever the number divides evenly.
• Repeat the process until the remaining value is itself prime.
• Display the complete prime factorization in standard form.
