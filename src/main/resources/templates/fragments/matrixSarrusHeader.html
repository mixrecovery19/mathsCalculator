Calculating the Determinant of a 3 × 3 Matrix using the Sarrus Method

The determinant helps determine whether a matrix can be inverted.

For a \(3 \times 3\) matrix, the determinant can be calculated using the Sarrus Method.

Given a matrix:\[A=\begin{bmatrix}a & b & c \\d & e & f \\ g & h & i \end{bmatrix} \] the determinant is calculated by summing the downward diagonals and subtracting the upward diagonals: \[\det(A)=aei+bfg+cdh - ceg-bdi-afh\] In essence, the Sarrus method is a sequence of elementary multiplication and addition/subtraction steps performed in order, allowing the determinant of a \(3 \times 3\) matrix to be calculated manually in an efficient and visual way.
