Solving Simultaneous Linear Equations Using Matrices

A pair of simultaneous equations can be written as a matrix equation.

For example,\[\begin{aligned}ax+by&=c\\dx+ey&=f\end{aligned}\] can be written in matrix form as \[\begin{bmatrix}a & b\\d & e\end{bmatrix}\times\begin{bmatrix}x\\y\end{bmatrix} =\begin{bmatrix}c\\f\end{bmatrix}\] Throughout this walkthrough you will:

• Enter the coefficients for your equations.
• Build the coefficient matrix.
• Calculate its determinant.
• Find the inverse matrix.
• Multiply the inverse by the constants vector.
• Calculate the values of \(x\) and \(y\).
