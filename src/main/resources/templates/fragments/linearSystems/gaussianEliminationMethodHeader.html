Solving Simultaneous Linear Equations Using Gaussian Elimination

A pair of simultaneous linear equations can be written as an augmented matrix.
For example, \[\begin{aligned}ax+by&=c\\dx+ey&=f\end{aligned}\] becomes \[\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}a & b & c\\d & e & f\end{array}\right]\] Gaussian Elimination solves the system by applying elementary row operations until the solution can be read directly.
Throughout this walkthrough you will:
• Enter the coefficients for your equations.
• Construct the augmented matrix.
• Apply elementary row operations.
• Eliminate one variable.
• Use back substitution to solve the remaining variable.
• Calculate the values of \(x\) and \(y\).
