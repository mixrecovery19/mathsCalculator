2. Perform the First Row Operation

The augmented matrix has now been constructed. Our next goal is to eliminate the coefficient beneath the first pivot by performing an elementary row operation. Follow the walkthrough below to perform the first elimination step.

Why do we perform row operations?

Gaussian Elimination transforms the augmented matrix into row-echelon form. For a \(2\times2\) system, our first objective is to make the lower-left entry equal to zero. Suppose the augmented matrix is \[\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} a&b&c\\d&e&f\end{array}\right]\] We calculate the elimination factor \[k=\frac{d}{a}\] and then replace Row 2 with \[R_2 \leftarrow R_2-kR_1\] This removes the coefficient beneath the pivot.
