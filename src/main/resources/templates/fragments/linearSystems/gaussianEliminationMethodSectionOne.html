1. Construct the Augmented Matrix

Building the Augmented Matrix

Your simultaneous equations are now combined into a single augmented matrix.

The first two columns contain the coefficients of x and y, while the final column contains the constants from the right-hand side of each equation.

Gaussian Elimination works by applying elementary row operations to this augmented matrix until the solution can be read directly.

The augmented matrix is
\[ \left[ \begin{array}{cc|c} a & b & c\\ d & e & f \end{array} \right] \]
