You can now click through each step of the determinant calculation for a \(2 \times 2\) matrix.

Given the matrix:\[A=\begin{bmatrix}a & b \\c & d\end{bmatrix}\] the determinant is calculated using: \[\det(A)=ad-bc\] In matrices, we always refer torows first and thencolumns.

This means:\[a=A_{11},\quad b=A_{12},\quad c=A_{21}, \quad d=A_{22}\] The determinant is calculated in three stages: \[\text{Positive diagonal: } a \times d\] \[\text{Negative diagonal: } b \times c\] \[\det(A) = (a \times d) - (b \times c)\] This walkthrough allows you to calculate the determinant visually, step by step.

Step Process To Calculate the 2 × 2 Inverse Matrix

Now that the determinant has been calculated, we can continue to calculate the inverse of the \(2 \times 2\) matrix.

Given the matrix:\[A=\begin{bmatrix}a & b \\c & d\end{bmatrix}\] the inverse is calculated using: \[A^{-1}=\frac{1}{\det(A)}\begin{bmatrix}d & -b \\-c & a\end{bmatrix}\] This walkthrough first swaps the main diagonal values, then changes the signs of the off-diagonal values, then applies the determinant.
